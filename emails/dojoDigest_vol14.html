Updates

October saw several improvements to the site. The development of the heatmap has had a huge impact on the habits of the dojo. Sensei Kraai no longer tallies his work and puts it in on a Friday. No, now that crusty old man enters his work every day. And there’s a feeling of responsibility about it. A day off feels wrong. The dojo most clearly sees it in the graduation shows (twitch.tv/chessdojo at 12 ET every Wednesday and Thursday!), those who are graduating have been especially committed to a daily routine. We also introduced Stockfish to the Dojo. The primary purpose of this is for players to do opening work (remember: no opening work until 1100) and is part of our long-term goal of creating a space where higher rated players can go to do all of their chess work. The next steps of this dream are to 1) perfect our files and Database system and 2) build an opening trainer. Once we have these, we will be able to offer the functionality of both Chessbase and Chessable for a fraction of the price. We brought the engine in with some reluctance, however. The dojo has had a wonderful culture of game analysis without the engine. And we hope the temptation of an engine does not hurt that. It is ok to check some positions from your games with the engine – but don’t do it until you have looked at that position critically with your own eyes, and always say what you saw and what the engine saw. Our stockfish is fancy! Here are some of the features we offer:

- You can view the engine's expected Win/Draw/Loss percentages under the main eval. In the engine settings, there is an option called Primary Eval Type. You can use this to display WDL percentages next to each line instead of the normal numeric eval.

- Clicking on the eval box next to a line will add that full line to your PGN and (by default) insert a comment with the engine name, eval, depth and WDL percentages. You can disable that comment from the engine settings. There is also a setting to enable that comment when clicking on a specific move in an engine line (which is off by default). Thanks to @mattchess mattchessic (C, L, -7) for suggesting/inspiring this.

- There is now an option in the engine settings and the game viewer settings to highlight the full engine line in the PGN text, in addition to the existing fish icon that displays at the start of the line. This setting is enabled by default.
